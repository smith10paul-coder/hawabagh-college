Collage Introductions | महाविद्यालय - परिचय

Hawabagh College, founded in 1928; stands near the bank of river Narmada with its majestic building and a beautiful campus of natural scenic beauty away from the noisy anal polluted environment of the city. The college is administered and maintained by the Methodist Church in India and has acquired its name from it's locale "Hawabagh." The collage is the Pioneer institute for education in Madhya Pradesh. The foundation of this noble institution was laid when Miss. E.L. Clinton, the then principal of Johnson Girls School felt the need for English teacher's. training, and within 10 months with the Government recognition opened Hawabagh Teachers training Collage with Ms. Hildegard Schlemmer as the first principal. The classes were held in the portico of the missionary bungalow under the banyan tree with just two students and teachers. This was the humble beginning of this great college in the embryonic stage where the campus today is clotted with majestic buildings, administrative blocks, assembly hall, library, gymnasium, Suman Bhawan, Women's Hostel, common room, canteen and a large play ground.

The institution has revolutionized women's education by fortifying its students in the cultural and the productive aspects drawing out the best in body, mind and spirit since its inception.

Traditions in the college campus have become sacred through accumulated traditional practices like regular Chapel which has distinguished it from it's kind, moral and religious preaching in the Chapel caters to the all round growth and development of the students with the necessary spiritual buildup.

“Mission Statement"

It is laid down in the Memorandum and Constitution of the Governing body of the Educational institutions of Madhya Pradesh Regional Conference of the Methodist Church of India.

The aims and objectives of the society shall be as under:

  • To administer, maintain and promote educational institutions established under the Madhya Pradesh Regional Conference of the Methodist Church in India.
  • To impart education, especially value-baseal, to provide sound learning, to inculcate spiritual truths and to build good moral character of its students so as to produce worthy citizens of India.
  • To spread goodwill among the students and all stakeholders, to provide edtucation and to open educational institutions primarily for the benefit and privilege of the christian minority community, nevertheless, exercising all other benefits and privileges granted to it as a minority institution by the constitution of India and through the various decisions made thereunder by the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India.

College Growth at a Glance

1928 Teachers Training College.
1939 Hawabagh Teacher's Institute affiliated to Nagpur University.
1946 Affiliated to University of Sagar.
1051 Department of Nursery Education.
1955 Bachelor in Teaching Course, College renamed as Hawabagh Teachers Training College.
1959 Addition of Arts and Science faculties.
1961 Hawabagh Teachers Training college changed to Hawabagh Women's college affiliated to University of Jabalpur (M.P.)
1962 Spacious Library and auditorium were constructed.
1963 Master's Degree In Education (M.ed.) introduced.
1966 Addition of Science Block and a large Gymnasium to the college Campus.
1978 Golden Jubilee Celebration.
1984 M.Sc. Chemistry introduced.
1995 M.A. English Literature introduced.
1996 College was recognized by University Gorant Commission Under 2(f) & 12(b).
1996 Commerce Faculty with Computer Application introduced.
1999 M.A. Economics, Geography and political science introduced.
2006 BBA, BCA and B.Sc. Biotechnology introduced.
2008 M. Com, introduced.
2008 Hawabagh Women's College changed to Hawabagh College Jabalpur. (M.P.)
2020 NEP was introduced at the undergraduate level for B.A., B.Sc., B.Com, and B.B.A.
